Understanding GST Registration Process: A Complete Guide

By CA Poonia

Published on March 15, 2024
8 min read

GST registration is a crucial step for businesses in India. This comprehensive guide will walk you through the entire process, required documents, and important considerations.

Table of Contents

  • 1. GST Registration Eligibility
  • 2. Required Documents
  • 3. Registration Process
  • 4. Timeline and Fees
  • 5. Post-Registration Compliance

1. GST Registration Eligibility

Businesses must register for GST if they meet any of the following criteria:

  • Annual turnover exceeds ₹20 lakhs (₹10 lakhs for special category states)
  • Engaged in inter-state supply of goods or services
  • E-commerce operators and suppliers
  • Casual taxable persons

2. Required Documents

Essential documents for GST registration include:

  • PAN card of the business
  • Aadhaar card of the proprietor/partners/directors
  • Proof of business registration
  • Bank account details
  • Address proof of the business

3. Registration Process

The GST registration process involves the following steps:

  1. Visit the GST portal (www.gst.gov.in)
  2. Fill out the registration form (GST REG-01)
  3. Submit required documents
  4. Verification of application
  5. Issuance of GSTIN

4. Timeline and Fees

Important timelines to remember:

  • Application processing: 3-7 working days
  • GST registration is free of cost
  • Late registration may attract penalties

5. Post-Registration Compliance

After obtaining GST registration, businesses must:

  • File regular GST returns
  • Maintain proper books of accounts
  • Issue tax invoices
  • Pay GST on time
